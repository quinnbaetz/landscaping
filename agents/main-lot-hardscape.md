# Main lot — Phase 1: Hardscape

## Principle

**Lot interior is build-up only.** Existing clay subgrade across the lot is left undisturbed. The one exception is a narrow toe trench at each wall line (~6 in deep × ~14 in wide) so the first block course is embedded below outside grade — locks the toe against sliding under fill pressure.

## Pre-work

- Clear leftover construction debris off the lot
- Lightly scarify (rake/rototill) top 1–2 in of clay so first lift keys in

## Fill (~95 cu yd total)

| Layer | Volume | Material |
|---|---|---|
| Structural fill (bottom) | ~45 cu yd | On-site stockpiled clay, 6–8 in compacted lifts |
| Topsoil cap (top) | ~50 cu yd | Imported sandy loam + compost, 12–24 in thick (12 in over central lawn, 16–24 in in 6 ft perimeter planting band) |

Average fill ~1.5 ft. South end: just cap (existing already at finish). North end: ~3 ft total.

## Layering & compaction

1. Scarify existing surface 1–2 in
2. Build walls + gravel drain column FIRST (before clay fill goes against them)
3. Moisture-condition clay to optimum (squeezed ball holds, no smear)
4. Place 6–8 in loose lifts (compact to ~5–6 in)
5. **Jumping-jack (rammer) compactor only** — plate compactor will not consolidate clay
6. 4–6 passes per lift, perpendicular directions
7. Field check: heel-print ≤ ¼ in deep
8. Build the 1° finish slope into the top of structural fill, not just the cap
9. Cap goes on top with light compaction only (root zones need air)

## Finish slope

- 1° south → north (~1 ft drop over 60 ft, ~2%)
- South edge flush with patio/house; water sheds toward north wall

## French drain (behind every wall)

- **Toe trench / base:** 4–6 in compacted ¾ in-minus gravel in the wall's toe trench. Beds both the wall and the drain pipe.
- **Perforated pipe:** 4 in perforated PVC at the base of the drain rock, sloped ~1% along the wall run, draining to a controlled daylight outlet at the NE corner. Cleanout risers at NW and SE ends of the pipe run.
- **Drain rock column:** clean ¾ in drain rock (washed, angular, no fines). **Width: 18 in along the north wall; 12 in for east, west, diagonal walls.** Terminates 12–16 in below finished grade so the cap above stays plantable.
- **Filter fabric:** non-woven geotextile or biodegradable natural-fiber felt — contractor's choice. Wraps the soil side and the top of the gravel column.
- **Outlet:** single controlled outlet through the wall at the NE corner. Pipe daylights onto a rock-apron splash pad on the driveway side. Block-course gaps along the rest of the wall remain as backup overflow.
- **Surface swale:** shallow turf swale along the south edge of the north planting band (~6 in × ~18 in), sloped ~1% east toward the NE corner. Collects surface runoff from the lawn before it pools at the wall; routes it to the same NE-corner outlet via a small surface inlet.

## Long-term drainage notes

- Rock-apron splash pad on the driveway side of the NE-corner outlet (catches any sediment carried out by the pipe)
- Year-10 maintenance: inspect outlet clarity in heavy rain; rebuild drain section if running muddy

## Topsoil sourcing

| Yard | Location | Order |
|---|---|---|
| American Soil & Stone | Richmond | "Diestel Structured Compost" + sandy loam custom blend, ~60/40 loam-to-compost |
| Acapulco Rock & Soil | Pleasant Hill (closest) | "Garden Mix" or "Planting Mix" |
| Lyngso | San Carlos (~45 min) | Vegetable Garden Mix or custom 50/50 sandy loam + compost |
| Grab N Grow / Soil Tech | Walnut Creek / Concord | Backup |

**Spec:** sandy loam (NOT generic "topsoil"), **20–30% organic matter by volume** (lower than vegetable spec — better for fruit trees), screened ½ in minus, pH 6.0–7.0, no biosolids, no construction-screened fill. Reject free Craigslist fill (usually clay spoils).

## Material staging

- Stockpile zones on driveway with plywood under each pile
- Sequence: drain rock + wall block first (build walls + drain first), then structural fill clay, then topsoil cap last (so it doesn't sit getting compacted/weedy)

## Timing

- Best earthworks season: **August–October** (Bay Area clay at optimum moisture)
- Winter unsuitable (too wet for compaction)
- Plant in following Jan–Feb (bare-root window)
